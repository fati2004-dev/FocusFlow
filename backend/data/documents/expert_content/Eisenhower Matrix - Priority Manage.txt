Eisenhower Matrix - Priority Management Method

## Overview
The Eisenhower Matrix, also known as the Urgent-Important Matrix, is a time management tool for prioritizing tasks by urgency and importance. It was popularized by Stephen Covey and attributed to President Dwight D. Eisenhower.

## The Four Quadrants

### Quadrant 1: DO FIRST (Urgent + Important)
- **Description**: Tasks that require immediate attention and have significant consequences
- **Examples**: Crises, deadlines, emergencies, pressing problems
- **Action**: Do these tasks immediately and personally
- **Strategy**: Minimize time in this quadrant by preventing crises before they occur

### Quadrant 2: SCHEDULE (Not Urgent + Important)
- **Description**: Activities that contribute to long-term goals and personal development
- **Examples**: Planning, relationship building, learning new skills, exercise, strategic thinking
- **Action**: Schedule time for these tasks, they are key to long-term success
- **Strategy**: Spend most time here - this is where effectiveness lives

### Quadrant 3: DELEGATE (Urgent + Not Important)
- **Description**: Interruptions that demand attention but don't contribute to goals
- **Examples**: Some emails, unnecessary meetings, some phone calls, interruptions
- **Action**: Delegate to others when possible, minimize time spent here
- **Strategy**: Learn to say no and set boundaries

### Quadrant 4: ELIMINATE (Not Urgent + Not Important)
- **Description**: Time-wasting activities that provide no value
- **Examples**: Mindless scrolling, excessive TV, gossip, busywork
- **Action**: Eliminate completely from your schedule
- **Strategy**: Recognize and avoid time traps

## How to Apply
1. List all your tasks
2. Assess each task: Is it urgent? Is it important?
3. Place each task in the appropriate quadrant
4. Take action: Do First → Schedule → Delegate → Eliminate
